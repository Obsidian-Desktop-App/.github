## 🚀 Overview
Obsidian for Windows is a note-taking app that keeps your writing in plain Markdown files stored right on your PC. Instead of scattering ideas across folders, you link notes to one another so your obsidian notes grow into a navigable web of knowledge. Because every file lives locally, your data stays private and portable, and you can extend the app with obsidian plugins to fit the way you think and work. Whether you are capturing quick thoughts, building study materials, or organizing a research project, Obsidian gives writers, students, and knowledge workers a durable home for their notes.

## ✨ Features
- **Local Markdown files** — Every note is a portable obsidian md file on your own disk, so you keep full ownership and control of your content.
- **Bidirectional linking** — Connect related notes with internal links and backlinks to build a genuine knowledge base rather than isolated pages.
- **Graph view** — Visualize how your obsidian notes relate to one another and discover connections you might have missed.
- **Plugin ecosystem** — Add capabilities through obsidian plugins, from task management to spaced repetition, and tailor the app to your workflow.
- **Fast search and organization** — Find anything instantly with quick switching, tags, folders, and full-text search across your entire vault.

## ❓ FAQ
**Is Obsidian free?**
Obsidian is free to use for personal note-taking. You own your files outright, and optional add-on services are available separately if you ever want them.

**Which versions of Windows are supported?**
Obsidian runs on 64-bit Windows 10 and Windows 11. Keeping Windows updated is recommended for the best experience.

**How does Obsidian vs Notion compare for note-taking?**
Obsidian stores your notes as local Markdown files that you fully control, while a tool like Notion is cloud-first. If offline access, file ownership, and an extensible local knowledge base matter to you, Obsidian is designed around exactly those priorities.

**Is my data secure?**
Yes. Your notes are stored as plain files on your own Windows PC, so nothing has to leave your machine for the app to work, and you decide if and how to back them up.
